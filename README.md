# 人类体验系统模型：调节层、体验层与叙事层的统一结构

A Systems Model of Human Experience: A Unified Structural Account of Regulatory, Experiential, and Narrative Layers

---

## 适用范围说明

Scope Note

本模型用于描述**人类体验在调节层、体验层与叙事层之间的结构关系及其触发条件**。它关注的是体验如何在不同层级被处理、终止或升级，而非对具体心理内容、人格类型或临床诊断作分类判断。

该模型适用于理解个体层面的体验生成机制，也可作为分析社会、文化或技术系统中叙事形成与失配现象的结构框架。它并不试图替代既有心理学、神经科学或哲学理论，而是为这些理论提供一个**层级清晰、条件明确的系统坐标**。

This model is intended to describe the structural relationships and triggering conditions across regulatory, experiential, and narrative layers of human experience. Its focus lies in how experience is processed, terminated, or escalated across layers, rather than in categorizing specific psychological contents, personality traits, or clinical diagnoses.

The model applies to individual-level experience generation and can also serve as a structural framework for analyzing narrative formation and misalignment in social, cultural, or technological systems. It does not seek to replace existing theories in psychology, neuroscience, or philosophy, but instead offers a system-level coordinate framework with clear layering and conditional logic.

---

## 核心结论

Core Thesis

人类体验并非必然从感受走向情绪、再走向叙事与语言。多数体验在调节层或体验层即已完成处理。只有当调节失败、体验持续存在，且系统进入自反性评估并遭遇结构性处理瓶颈时，叙事层才会被激活。情绪、意图与语言由此出现，作为系统应对复杂位势差的压缩性工具，而非体验的原始起点。

Human experience does not inevitably progress from sensation to emotion, and then to narrative and language. Most experiences are resolved within regulatory or experiential layers. Narrative processing is activated only when regulation fails, experience persists, and the system enters reflexive evaluation under structural processing constraints. Emotion, intent, and language emerge under these conditions as compressive tools for handling complex potential gradients, rather than as the original source of experience.

## 阅读说明

How to Read This Model

本模型不是线性流程，而是一个具有分叉、回落与条件升级机制的系统结构。

情绪与语言并非必然结果，而是在特定结构条件下被激活的功能层。

This model does not represent a linear process, but a system with bifurcation, fallback, and conditional escalation mechanisms.

Emotion and language are not inevitable outcomes, but functional layers activated under specific structural conditions.

## 模型术语定义

Glossary of Model Terms

---

### Physical / Biological World

物理 / 生物 / 社会结构世界

Objective conditions that exist independently of perception, including environment, bodily states, other agents, and social or structural positions.

独立于主观体验而存在的客观条件，包括环境、身体状态、他人以及社会或结构位置。

---

### Hormonal / Neural Signals

激素 / 神经信号

Biological signaling processes through which external or internal conditions influence the organism’s internal state.

外在或内在条件通过生物机制影响机体内部状态的信号过程。

---

### System State Change

系统状态改变

Physiological or neural deviations within the organism caused by hormonal or neural signaling.

由激素或神经信号引发的生理或神经层面的状态变化。

---

### Tension Gradient

张力差 / 稳态偏离

A purely physiological deviation from homeostasis, such as hunger, pain, fatigue, or threat, without consciousness or meaning.

系统稳态的生理性偏离，如饥饿、疼痛、疲劳或威胁，不具备意识或意义。

---

### Not Perceived

未被感知

A condition in which tension gradients are resolved through automatic regulation or direct action without entering subjective experience.

张力差通过自动调节或直接行动被解决，未进入主观体验的状态。

---

### Automatic Regulation

自动调节

System-level corrective processes that restore equilibrium without conscious involvement.

无需意识参与即可恢复系统稳态的调节过程。

---

### Direct Action

直接行动

Immediate behavioral responses that reduce tension gradients without reflective processing.

无需反思即可减少张力差的即时行为反应。

---

### Perceived

被感知

A condition in which a tension gradient enters subjective awareness and becomes experiential.

张力差进入主观觉察并成为体验的状态。

---

### Pre-Narrative Affect

原始情绪

The felt form of tension when perceived, without object, meaning, self-reference, or narrative structure.

张力被感知后的体验形态，但尚无对象、意义、自我指向或叙事结构。

---

### Regulation Check

调节判断

The system’s evaluation of whether regulation alone can resolve perceived tension.

系统对是否仅凭调节即可消解已被感知张力的判断过程。

---

### Regulation Succeeds

调节成功

A condition in which perceived tension is resolved through physiological or behavioral regulation, terminating further processing.

被感知的张力通过生理或行为调节被消解，系统不再升级处理。

---

### Regulation Fails

调节失败

A condition in which perceived tension persists despite regulatory attempts.

在调节尝试后张力仍持续存在的状态。

---

### Cognitive Surplus

大脑容量冗余

The human cognitive capacity to represent and process internal states beyond immediate regulation.

人类认知系统超出即时调节需求、能够表征自身状态的能力。

---

### Reflexivity

自反性

The system’s ability to observe and evaluate its own state, enabling comparison between current and alternative states.

系统对自身状态进行观察与评估，并比较当前状态与其他可能状态的能力。

---

### Potential Gradient

位势差

A structural asymmetry between the current state and evaluated alternatives, prior to language or narrative.

当前状态与被评估状态之间的结构性不对称，存在于语言与叙事之前。

---

### Potential Gradient Axis Space

位势差轴空间

The multi-dimensional evaluative space formed by evaluative, social, and temporal axes prior to narrative selection.

在叙事选择之前，由评价轴、社会轴与时间轴构成的多维评估空间。

---

### Structural Processing Bottleneck

结构性处理瓶颈

The limitation of human cognition in resolving multiple potential gradients in parallel.

人类认知无法并行消解多维位势差的结构性限制。

---

### Narrativity Interface

叙事性接口

A compression mechanism that reduces structural complexity through perspective binding, axis selection, causal simplification, and temporal linearization.

通过视角绑定、主轴选择、因果简化与时间线性化来压缩结构复杂度的机制。

---

### Emotion

情绪

The experiential outcome of pre-narrative affect after narrative binding, naming, and interpretation.

原始情绪在叙事绑定、命名与解释之后形成的体验状态。

---

### Intent Formation

意图生成

The formation of directional action schemas that guide behavior following narrative compression.

叙事压缩后形成的、用于指导行为的定向行动方案。

---

### Language / Symbolic Externalization

语言 / 符号外显

The external expression of narrative structures in communicable symbolic form.

叙事结构以可交流的符号形式被外化的过程。

---

### Narrative Alignment Test

叙事对齐测试

The process by which narrative structures are tested against external reality and social coordination.

叙事结构与现实条件及社会协作进行匹配与验证的过程。

---

### Anxiety

焦虑

A state arising from persistent misalignment between narrative structures and unresolved potential gradients.

叙事结构与未解决位势差持续失配时产生的状态。

## 结语

Closing Note

本模型提出了一种统一的人类体验系统结构，用以描述调节层、体验层与叙事层之间的关系及其运行条件。通过区分张力调节、被感知体验与叙事压缩三个层级，模型说明了人类体验并非必然沿着单一路径展开，而是在多个关键节点上发生分叉、终止或升级。

该模型的价值不在于重新定义某一种心理现象，而在于提供一套结构化框架，使不同层级的体验过程能够被清晰区分、准确定位，并在同一系统中被理解。由此，人类体验可以被视为一个条件驱动、可回落、非线性的系统，而非以情绪或语言为中心的连续过程。

This model presents a unified systems structure of human experience, describing the relationships and operating conditions across regulatory, experiential, and narrative layers. By distinguishing between tension regulation, perceived experience, and narrative compression, the model demonstrates that human experience does not unfold along a single inevitable pathway, but instead bifurcates, terminates, or escalates at multiple critical points.

The contribution of this model lies not in redefining individual psychological phenomena, but in offering a structural framework that allows different experiential layers to be clearly distinguished, precisely located, and understood within the same system. Human experience is thus conceptualized as a condition-driven, non-linear system with fallback mechanisms, rather than a process centered on emotion or language.
